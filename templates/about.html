Who we are

Our Total Christ Spirituality

All about Christ — his work, not ours

We want this to be all about Christ — his work, not ours, as the basis of our relationship to God and one another, and his glory, not any popular leader, as the object of our ultimate affection. It is our desire to experience total Christ, not just one brand of Christ.

The Word was made flesh, and dwelled among us; to that flesh is joined the church, and there is made total Christ, both head and body.

— St. Augustine, 5th-century pastor-theologian

Our distinctives

The Five Marks

Our distinctive approach to church life

Gospel-Centered

Trust in the gospel makes us humble, because Christ's work to us is a gift, yet boldly confident, because it's grounded in God's power. Everything we do flows from the finished work of the cross.

Missional

God promises to dwell with his people across every culture and language through the church, so we strive to be missional — open, inviting, and purposeful in showing the world who Jesus is.

Confessional

We humbly submit to God's Word as it has been historically interpreted, subscribing to the Westminster Standards as our summary of faith — doctrine that is alive, not distracted by cultural fads.

Sacramental

Christ is fleshed out in a worship service that follows the logic of the gospel and culminates each week in communion — friendly, worshipful, and mysterious.

Communal

Christ joined us to himself and to his church, so to be a Christian is to belong to a redeemed community — marked by loving, humble accountability and one-anothering.

Our faith

What We Believe

The doctrines at the heart of who we are

We believe the Bible is God's authoritative and inerrant revelation to us, speaking with clarity and power to every area of our lives and faith. We believe salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ alone. Christ's death and resurrection reconciles us to God, and nothing we do can earn or add to this gift. We believe God is sovereign over all creation and history, accomplishing his purpose through Christ, who will consummate all things and rule forever.

We believe the Holy Spirit dwells in and empowers believers, producing fruit in our lives and gifting us for service in God's kingdom. We believe the church is Christ's body, called to make disciples, advance his kingdom, and show the world what it means to follow Jesus. We believe we are called to pursue holiness, live out the gospel in community, and advance God's mission in the world.

We believe believers will spend eternity in the presence of God in the new heavens and new earth, and that God's kingdom will ultimately be consummated when Christ returns in glory. Until that day, we are committed to worshiping God, growing in faith, and extending God's love and justice to our community and the world.
