What We Believe

Christ Presbyterian Church holds to the historic Christian faith as summarized in the Scriptures and expressed in the Reformed confessions.

Scripture

Our final authority

We believe the Bible is the inspired, inerrant, and sufficient Word of God. It is our supreme authority in all matters of faith and life.

God

One God in three persons

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—equal in power and glory, and worthy of the same worship and obedience.

Christ

Lord and Savior

We believe Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, who became man, lived a sinless life, died for our sins, rose from the dead, and will return in glory. Salvation is found in him alone.

The Church

God’s people in the world

We believe the church is the body of Christ, called to worship, grow in grace, and make disciples. Christ Presbyterian Church is a congregation of the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA), committed to the Westminster Standards.

Confessional Standards

What we teach and confess

Our pastors and elders subscribe to the Westminster Confession of Faith and the Larger and Shorter Catechisms as faithful summaries of biblical doctrine. We welcome anyone seeking to know more—visit, ask questions, and explore with us.

School of Discipleship class

Sundays 9/17/23 – 5/19/24 at 9:30am in the Fellowship Hall.

No class: Thanksgiving weekend 11/26/23, winter recess 12/17/23 – 12/31/23, or Easter Sunday 3/31/24.

We’re called to not just know about God, but to know God personally. This class walks through the historic Christian beliefs we hold to at CPC—grappling with why they’re important, where they come from, and how we can grow in our love for the Lord and His Word. Much more than just a lecture, we discuss real-life implications of how our theology can inform our spiritual, emotional, and relational health, to the glory of God and the building of His Church. This class also serves as the requirement for teaching Sunday School and other leadership roles at CPC.
